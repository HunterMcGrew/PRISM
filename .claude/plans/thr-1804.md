# Plan: THR-1804 — Tighten DX ticket-type definition in ticket-types.md

## Ticket

[THR-1804](https://linear.app/tractru/issue/THR-1804/tighten-dx-ticket-type-definition-in-ticket-typesmd)

## Goal

Tighten the DX entry in `.claude/templates/ticket-types.md` so the boundary against Improvement is decidable on read — lead with the negative test, name the colloquial-vs-categorical trap explicitly, and give the reader a one-line decision rule plus a fast reframe question. Collapse the DX line in `.claude/architect/skills-ecosystem.md` § Ticket Types to a one-liner that points at the template, so the ecosystem doc and the template can't drift.

---

## Context

THR-1803 surfaced the failure mode: Nora pattern-matched a docs-only/architect/ADR ticket to Improvement because the colloquial sense of "improvement" (the codebase is being made better) won against the categorical rule (does the work produce observable behavior change a tester can verify?). The current DX one-liner in `ticket-types.md` is precise but compressed — the negative test that distinguishes DX from Improvement is buried at the end of the sentence, and the seven-example noun catalog reads as a list to match against rather than a rule to apply.

Per ADR-0004, templates are canonical. The fix lands in `ticket-types.md` (the source of truth) and the ecosystem doc collapses to a pointer — no duplicated definition to drift.

## Decisions

- **Clove executes both edits as a single voice-coherent pass** — the two files read together (the ecosystem doc points at the template), so one author keeps the framing consistent. Considered: splitting authorship across personas. Rejected: voice drift between a definition and the doc that points at it would defeat the consolidation goal.
- **Two examples, not zero** — the ticket prescribes two, and the writing-voice rule allows enumerated lists when they illustrate without becoming a noun catalog. Considered: deleting examples entirely and relying on the decision rule alone. Rejected per the ticket's explicit "single concrete reframe" + "two examples" requirements; one decision rule plus two illustrations is more grounded than the rule alone.
- **Examples chosen are architect docs/ADRs and CI/CD/build tooling** — these are the two strongest fits for the decision rule (no dealer-visible behavior, but meaningfully improve the codebase). The ticket text names these two specifically. Considered: keeping devcontainer or test infrastructure. Rejected: both are correctly DX, but two examples land harder than four when the goal is teaching the rule, not enumerating cases.
- **Ecosystem doc collapses to a pointer, not a duplicated definition** — per ADR-0004 (templates are canonical). The current ecosystem line trails `No user stories, no AC.` after the definition; that detail lives in the template's metadata bullets, so it's safe to drop from the pointer. Considered: keeping the trailing fragment in the ecosystem doc. Rejected: every word in the ecosystem doc that restates a template detail is a future drift risk.
- **No promotion to architect context** — this change tightens an existing definition; it doesn't establish a new pattern. The "categorical vs colloquial" framing is local to the DX/Improvement boundary, not a general rule to lift.
