## Debugged Issues

### LocalVideo does not support fillContainer prop
- **Status:** `fixed`
- **Fixed in:** `VideoComponentFactory.tsx` â€” now passes `fillContainer` to `LocalVideo`. `LocalVideo` conditionally uses `absolute inset-0 h-full w-full object-cover` when `fillContainer` is true, otherwise keeps `aspect-video h-full w-full object-cover`.
- **Severity:** High
- **Environment:** Frontend â€” Panel Hero block with local (self-hosted) video
- **File:** `frontend/components/panel-hero/VideoComponentFactory.tsx:36`
- **Root cause:** `VideoComponentFactory` destructures `fillContainer` out of props at line 28 but does not pass it to `LocalVideo` at line 36 (`...props` is the rest object which excludes `fillContainer`). Furthermore, `LocalVideo` does not accept or handle a `fillContainer` prop at all â€” it always renders a `<video>` with `className="aspect-video h-full w-full object-cover"` which uses aspect-ratio sizing instead of filling the parent container.
- **Steps to Reproduce:**
  1. Add a Panel Hero block with a local (self-hosted) video as the main panel media
  2. View the page on the frontend
  3. Observe the video does not fill the container â€” it renders at aspect-video ratio
- **Expected behavior:** Local video fills the entire panel with no gaps, same as Vimeo
- **Actual behavior:** Video renders at fixed 16:9 aspect ratio, does not fill non-16:9 containers
- **Recommended fix:** Two changes needed: (1) In `VideoComponentFactory`, pass `fillContainer` to `LocalVideo`. (2) In `LocalVideo`, when `fillContainer` is true, replace `aspect-video` with absolute positioning and `object-fit: cover` â€” unlike iframes, `object-fit: cover` works natively on `<video>` elements, so no oversized-element technique is needed.
- **Suggested tests:** Test `LocalVideo` renders with `object-cover` + absolute positioning when `fillContainer` is true. Test `VideoComponentFactory` passes `fillContainer` to all three video types.
- **Linear:** `not synced`

### YouTube videos â€” scale buffer applied in wrong place inside max()
- **Status:** `fixed`
- **Fixed in:** `FillContainerIframe.tsx` â€” moved `* 1.02` outside `max()` so both dimensions are always buffered: `calc(max(100cqw, 100cqh * 16 / 9) * 1.02)` / `calc(max(100cqh, 100cqw * 9 / 16) * 1.02)`. Large black bar on staging `/josue-testing` is a content issue (source video ~2.39:1), not a code defect.
- **Severity:** Medium
- **Environment:** Frontend â€” staging, Panel Hero with YouTube video at `/josue-testing` (~1232Ă—800px container)
- **File:** `frontend/components/thrive-video/FillContainerIframe.tsx:36-37`
- **Root cause:** Two issues: (1) The `* 1.02` scale buffer was placed inside the `max()` on only the 16:9 calculation branch. When `100cqh` wins the `max()` (container narrower than 16:9), the buffer has no effect. (2) The large black bar on staging is a content issue â€” source video `rbLAG7fr7tA` is ~2.39:1 cinematic widescreen, not 16:9.
- **Linear:** `synced`
